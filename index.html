About Me

I am fascinated at how technology has and is currently rapidly transforming the way we live with timeless, creative innovations. Being apart of something bigger than myself makes me enthusiastic to explore exciting industries and being apart of a place that turns ideas into reality.

I recently graduated with a BSME from PSU and am currently pursing opportunities within various industries. My education has given me a strong background in product design and analysis. I love to design, conceptualize, and make all sorts of things in my free time and see them through completion. As a mechanical engineering student the last several years, I have continiously improved myself academically and spiritually. I'm an Engineer-In-Training and my previous experiences have made me skilled in computer-aided design, finite element analysis and working in teams. Always seeking opportunities to work tirelessly on a team dedicated to make a tangible impact.

Apart from my technical side, I have a passion in the health and the well-being of others and myself as I had previously devoted my time as a student volunteer and in recreation.

Thermal Finite Element Analysis

Top level design requirements for fin design were to have adequate heat transfer and to meet the needs to an aesthetic feature that resembled the style of the custom motorcycle. The proposed 14-fin design has 85% overall fin surface efficiency compared to the 81% from the original V106 engine due to the changes in fin pitch, thickness, and spacing.

Impact FEA

Analyzed the section of a car roll cage for a frontal impact at a speed of 185 mph and investigated the behavior of the structure when the impact is with a solid wall versus the SAFER barrier. The SAFER barrier absorbs a significant amount of impact energy from the vehicle proving their worth being installed on all major NASCAR tracks.

Abaqus/CAE, 2D/3D, Explicit Method
